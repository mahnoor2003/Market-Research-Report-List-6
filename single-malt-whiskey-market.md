<p><p>Single malt whiskey is categorized into various market types based on geographic origin and production methods. Scotch whiskey is made in Scotland from malted barley and aged a minimum of three years, with distinct regional flavors. American whiskey encompasses styles like bourbon and rye, often characterized by sweeter profiles and the use of new charred oak barrels. Irish whiskey, typically smoother and triple-distilled, comes from Ireland and includes single pot still variations. Other markets include Japanese and Indian whiskeys, gaining global recognition for their craftsmanship.</p></p>
