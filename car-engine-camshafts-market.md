<p><p>The car engine camshaft market is primarily divided into flat-tappet and roller cam types. Flat-tappet cams feature a simpler design, utilizing a flat surface that makes contact with the engine's lifters, suitable for older or less powerful engines. In contrast, roller cams utilize a cylindrical roller for more efficient motion, reducing friction and wear, thus enhancing performance and longevity. The choice between these cam types affects engine performance, efficiency, and maintenance needs, catering to diverse automotive applications and preferences.</p></p>
