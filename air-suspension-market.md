<p><p>The air suspension market is primarily divided into two types: electronically controlled air suspension systems and non-electronically controlled air suspension systems. Electronically controlled systems use sensors and electronic control units to adjust air pressure and suspension settings dynamically, enhancing ride comfort and stability. Non-electronically controlled systems rely on mechanical components to maintain air pressure, providing a more straightforward and often less expensive solution. Both types serve various applications, from passenger vehicles to heavy-duty trucks, addressing diverse consumer needs in ride quality and load management.</p></p>
