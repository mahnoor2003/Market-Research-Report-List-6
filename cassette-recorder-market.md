<p><ul><li>Type I Cassettes</li><li>Type II Cassettes</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cassette recorder market primarily features two types of cassette tapes: Type I and Type II. Type I cassettes utilize ferric oxide tape, providing standard audio quality, making them suitable for everyday recording needs. Type II cassettes, known as chrome or CrO2 tapes, offer enhanced sound quality and reduced distortion, ideal for music and high-fidelity recordings. As digital music gained prominence, demand for both cassette types has diminished, but they still attract niche markets interested in retro technology and analog sound.</p></p>
