<p><p>The renewable naphtha market consists of two main types: light and heavy naphtha. Light naphtha typically has a lower boiling range and is used as a feedstock for petrochemical production, particularly in the manufacture of gasoline and chemicals. Heavy naphtha, on the other hand, has a higher boiling range and is primarily utilized in the production of diesel, jet fuel, and as a blendstock in refining processes. Both types are derived from renewable sources, contributing to sustainable fuel alternatives.</p></p>
