<p><p>The white marble market comprises two primary types: natural marble and artificial marble. Natural marble is a premium product, prized for its unique patterns and durability, sourced from quarries. It is often used in high-end applications like flooring and countertops. In contrast, artificial marble, or engineered stone, is crafted from a combination of resin and crushed stone, offering a more affordable, customizable option. While natural marble appeals to luxury markets, artificial marble caters to budget-conscious consumers seeking aesthetic versatility.</p></p>
