<p><p>Continuous level monitors are categorized into several market types based on their measurement principles. Differential pressure monitors gauge fluid levels by measuring pressure variations. Guided wave radar uses microwave signals to track levels, while non-contact radar employs microwaves without direct contact. Ultrasonic level sensors utilize sound waves to detect levels, and capacitance sensors measure changes in capacitance related to material presence. Other market types may include magnetostrictive and laser-based systems, each offering unique advantages for specific applications in level monitoring.</p></p>
