<p><p>The plastic containers market is segmented into various resin types: </p><p>1. **Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET)** - commonly used for food and beverage packaging due to its strength and recyclability. </p><p>2. **High Density Polyethylene (HDPE)** - known for its durability and chemical resistance; ideal for containers like milk jugs. </p><p>3. **Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC)** - used in plumbing and packaging, valued for its rigidity. </p><p>4. **Low Density Polyethylene (LDPE)** - flexible and lightweight, often used for bags and squeezable bottles. </p><p>5. **Polypropylene (PP)** - resistant to heat, suitable for containers used in microwave applications. </p><p>6. **Polystyrene (PS)** - utilized in disposable cups and containers; inexpensive but less durable. </p><p>7. **Other Resins** - includes various biodegradable and specialized plastics that meet niche market needs.</p></p>
