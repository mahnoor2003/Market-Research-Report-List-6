<p><p>The donor egg IVF treatment market consists of two primary types: fresh donor egg IVF cycles and frozen donor egg IVF cycles. Fresh donor egg IVF involves using eggs retrieved from a donor and immediately fertilized for the recipient, offering higher success rates but requiring synchronized donor and recipient cycles. In contrast, frozen donor egg IVF utilizes eggs that have been cryopreserved, allowing for flexible timing and broader access to donated eggs, although success rates may vary based on the freezing methods used.</p></p>
