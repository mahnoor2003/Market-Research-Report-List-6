<p><ul><li>Vertically Stacking</li><li>Photolithography</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The mobile NAND Flash market consists primarily of two types: vertically stacking and photolithography. Vertically stacking refers to the 3D arrangement of memory cells, allowing for higher density storage and improved performance while optimizing space utilization. Photolithography, on the other hand, involves traditional layer-by-layer chip fabrication techniques, enabling precise patterning of memory cells. Both methods aim to enhance data storage capabilities, efficiency, and lifespan, catering to the demands of mobile devices for faster and more reliable flash memory solutions.</p></p>
