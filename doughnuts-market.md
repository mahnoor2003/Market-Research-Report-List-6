<p><p>The doughnut market primarily features two styles: cake and yeast. Cake-style doughnuts are denser and richer, made from a batter that includes baking powder for leavening, resulting in a crumbly texture. They are often glazed or sprinkled and come in various flavors. In contrast, yeast-style doughnuts are lighter and airier, made with yeast to achieve a fluffy consistency. These doughnuts are often filled with cream or fruit preserves and have a soft, chewy bite, appealing to a different demographic in the market.</p></p>
