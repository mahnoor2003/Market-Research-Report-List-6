<p><p>The single-cell sequencing market comprises two main types: single-cell isolation and single-cell amplification. Single-cell isolation involves techniques to separate individual cells from a heterogeneous mixture, enabling detailed analysis of cellular heterogeneity. Single-cell amplification focuses on enhancing and replicating the genetic material from isolated single cells, ensuring sufficient material for sequencing. Together, these processes facilitate comprehensive study of cellular functions, genomics, and transcriptomics, driving advancements in personalized medicine, oncology, and developmental biology.</p></p>
