<p><p>The wool-protective agents market is categorized into two main types: ionic and nonionic agents. Ionic agents are charged molecules that interact strongly with wool fibers, providing effective protection against environmental damage and enhancing dye uptake. Nonionic agents, on the other hand, possess no electrical charge, allowing for gentler treatments that enhance the softness and manageability of wool without altering its natural properties. Both market types cater to different protective and aesthetic needs in wool processing and finishing.</p></p>
