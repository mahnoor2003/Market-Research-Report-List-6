<p><p>The retinal detachment treatment market includes various approaches aimed at reattaching the retina. Pneumatic retinopexy involves injecting a gas bubble into the eye to push the retina against the wall. Scleral buckle surgery entails placing a silicone band around the eye to support the retina. Vitrectomy involves removing the gel-like substance inside the eye to relieve traction and allow for retinal reattachment. Other treatments may include laser therapy and cryopexy, addressing unique patient needs and detachment types.</p></p>
