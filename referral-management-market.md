<p><p>Referral management encompasses three primary market types: self-referral, professional-referral, and third-party referral. Self-referral occurs when patients seek services directly based on their own research or experiences. Professional-referral involves healthcare providers directing patients to specialists for further care, often based on clinical needs. Third-party referrals are facilitated by external entities, such as insurance companies or health plans, which connect patients to appropriate providers based on coverage criteria or network availability. Each type plays a distinct role in patient navigation.</p></p>
