<p><p>The biomedical cryopreservation equipment market includes various types designed for specific temperature ranges and applications. Medical refrigerators maintain temperatures between 2℃ and 8℃ for short-term storage of sensitive medical supplies. Blood refrigerators operate at 4℃ with a ±1℃ tolerance for blood products. Medical freezers (-10℃ to -25℃) store biological samples long-term. Cryopreservation boxes (-10℃ to -50℃) provide controlled freezing, while cryogenic storage boxes (-50℃ to -150℃) handle ultra-low temperatures. Liquid nitrogen tanks (-150℃ to -196℃) are crucial for deep cryogenic storage. Other equipment may include specialized containers and monitoring systems.</p></p>
