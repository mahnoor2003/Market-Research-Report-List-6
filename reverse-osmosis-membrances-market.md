<p><p>The reverse osmosis membranes market includes two primary types: cellulose-based membranes and thin film composite membranes. Cellulose-based membranes are made from cellulose acetate, offering moderate filtration efficiency and lower costs, making them suitable for less demanding applications. In contrast, thin film composite membranes use advanced materials that provide higher separation efficiency and durability, allowing them to handle a wider range of contaminants and operating conditions. These membranes are widely used in various industries, including water treatment, food processing, and healthcare.</p></p>
