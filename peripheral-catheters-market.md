<p><strong>The Peripheral Catheters Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>PIVC</li><li>Midline Catheters</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Peripheral Intravenous Catheters (PIVC) are short catheters inserted into a vein in the arm or hand for short-term use, typically up to 72 hours. Midline catheters, on the other hand, are longer catheters that are placed in the arm and extend to the axilla, for use up to 4 weeks. Both types of catheters are used to administer medications, fluids, and blood products directly into the bloodstream. They are commonly used in hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare settings for patients who require frequent intravenous access.</p></p>
